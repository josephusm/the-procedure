# CONCEPT — The Procedure

## The idea

You are a functionary in an unnamed system.

Your job is to process requests. Every day, cases arrive on your desk: anomalies, complaints, exceptions, appeals. You read them. You decide what to do with them.

But you cannot refuse anything. The system does not have a "reject" option. It has *channels*. Every case has a correct channel. Your job is to find it and route accordingly.

The cases escalate. A citizen reports a disappearance. You route it to the Missing Persons Intake Form. A worker reports a safety violation. You route it to the Occupational Irregularity Register. A child reports that their school has been replaced by a facility they don't recognize. You route it to the Educational Infrastructure Review Queue.

Nothing is ever resolved. Everything is processed.

At some point — there is no clear moment — you realize that your own capacity to act outside the system has also been routed somewhere. You do not remember when. There was probably a form.

The game ends when you complete the final procedure. The final procedure is routine.

## Mechanics

- Text-based, browser-only. No canvas, no graphics.
- Each "day" presents one case. The player reads the case and chooses from a set of routing options.
- Routing options are always formally correct. There is no wrong answer. There is only the more correct channel.
- A hidden variable tracks the player's "Compliance Score" — never shown directly, but it affects which cases arrive and what options are available.
- As compliance increases, options narrow. The range of channels collapses toward fewer, more automatic choices.
- Late-game: the player has one option per case. The button still exists. The choice feels intact.
- Endgame: a final case arrives — the player's own case. They route it.

## Aesthetic

- Terminal interface: green text on near-black background.
- Monospace font throughout.
- No music, no animation. Cursor blink only.
- Timestamps on every entry. Everything is dated, logged, processed.
- The UI looks like a real internal system. Not a parody — a plausible one.
- Tone: dry bureaucratic English. No irony in the interface itself. The irony is structural.

## Inspiration

- Kafka's *The Trial* — the system that processes without resolving
- Papers, Please — complicity through procedure
- The "authorized corridor" as a concept: a system that does not suppress the exception but routes it into compatible form
- Neuromancer's Wintermute — not a break from the system but its perfection

## What this is not

- Not a satire with winking jokes
- Not a dystopia with a villain
- Not a game with a "good ending" where you resist
- The resistance option is also a channel

## Target

Anyone who has ever filed a complaint and watched it disappear into a queue.
Anyone who has ever been told their case is being handled.
Anyone who has worked inside a system long enough to stop noticing.
